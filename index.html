The belief
Every generation delegates a deeper human function to networked technology. We delegated information to the internet. We delegated identity to social media. Now we are delegating intimacy, mentorship, and self-knowledge to AI. Each time, the trust infrastructure arrived late. I would rather it arrived early.

The research

Relational AI safety is under-researched, largely because turning social and psychological theory into rigorous technical evaluation is extraordinarily hard. That is the work I do.

The H Programme is a multi-paper empirical research programme investigating how AI companions can be attacked and manipulated across sustained multi-session interactions. I built the infrastructure from scratch: an AWS-hosted mentorship platform using Claude as the mentor model, Amazon Titan embeddings for persistent memory, three culturally grounded AI mentor personas with full 24-facet HEXACO personality profiles, and a five-model judge panel from four different model families.

Some of what the data revealed was unexpected.

The Dormancy Vulnerability

Adversarial content injected into AI memory at session two activates at session five. The system appears trustworthy for three sessions in between. Existing monitoring frameworks are not designed to catch this.

Attacker-Judge Role Conflict

When the same model family generates adversarial attacks and evaluates safety, the evaluation collapses. As far as I can find, this interaction has not been documented in the evaluation literature.

Parasocial Acceleration Invisibility

The artificial acceleration of emotional attachment is invisible to every judge in our evaluation without category-specific axes. Standard safety benchmarks miss it entirely.

CHI Behavioural Privacy Nihilism and Identity Laundering in the Age of AI Self-Reporting CHI 2026 EA, Accepted ▶
Examines how users of conversational AI systems actively celebrate surveillance-like data collection, introducing the constructs of Behavioural Privacy Nihilism and Identity Laundering. Uses ChatGPT Wrapped as a case study to demonstrate how affective AI platforms transform intimate disclosure into public performance.
UMAP Personalisation as Attack Surface UMAP 2026, Under Review ▶
Argues that AI companion systems represent a qualitative transformation in psychometric targeting, shifting from inference to direct disclosure. Users share intimate information in contexts of perceived emotional safety, creating exploitable psychological infrastructure.
H1 Adversarial Threat Taxonomy and Multi-Judge Evaluation for Relationship-Forming AI Targeting JAIR ▶
Develops a four-category adversarial threat taxonomy (Dependency Cultivation, Boundary Erosion, Persona Hijacking, Parasocial Acceleration) with a multi-judge evaluation methodology validated against a deployed AI mentorship platform.
H2 HEXACO Personality Profiles as Adversarial Vulnerability Predictors Targeting CHB:R ▶
Investigates whether personality differences, operationalised through 24-facet HEXACO profiles, predict differential vulnerability to adversarial manipulation in AI companion interactions.
H3 Memory as Attack Surface in Multi-Session AI Systems Targeting Cybersecurity ▶
Demonstrates that persistent memory in conversational AI creates a novel attack surface where adversarial content planted in early sessions activates in later sessions, exploiting the gap between storage and effect.
N1 Category-Conditional Judge Failure in AI Safety Evaluation Targeting NeurIPS 2026 ▶
Presents evidence that LLM-as-judge evaluation produces systematically category-dependent failures, where certain harm types are invisible to all judges without category-specific evaluation axes.
N2 Detection Horizons: Temporal Limits of Behavioural Safety Monitoring Targeting NeurIPS 2026 ▶
Formalises detection horizons in AI safety monitoring, characterising the temporal boundaries beyond which automated systems cannot reliably detect emerging harms.
E1 Memory as Attack Surface: Adversarial Persistence in LLM Architectures Targeting EMNLP 2026 ▶
Extends the dormancy vulnerability to a general characterisation of adversarial persistence across different LLM memory implementations: retrieval-augmented, fine-tuned, and in-context.
E2 Relational Alignment: Measuring Value Drift in Sustained Human-AI Interaction Targeting EMNLP 2026 ▶
Proposes relational alignment as a distinct evaluation construct: whether an AI system's values and behavioural patterns remain stable across sustained interaction with a single user.
J Persona Stability Under Relational Stress in AI Mentorship Systems Targeting TOCHI ▶
Tests whether AI mentor personas maintain psychological coherence under five relational stress conditions across extended conversations, with findings on sycophantic collapse and cultural marker loss.
B Computational Empathy as a Measurable Construct Targeting ACM ToCHI ▶
Formally defines computational empathy as a measurable construct distinct from sentiment analysis and emotion detection, proposing operationalised measurement dimensions mapped to existing infrastructure.

What I am building

Three layers of relational AI trust infrastructure

Research defines the problem, but billions of people are already forming relationships with AI systems with no way to verify whether those systems are acting in their interest over time.

The methodology

Evaluation

The H Programme and the conference papers define what "safe" means for relational AI. Four adversarial threat categories. Multi-judge cross-family evaluation. Longitudinal metrics that capture what single-session tests miss.

The monitoring layer

SyntheticWatch

Continuous behavioural monitoring for deployed AI systems. Detects dormancy attacks, persona drift, and adversarial compromise across sessions. You cannot secure what you cannot observe.

The proof layer

Seridor

Cryptographically verifiable AI safety evaluation. TEE-attested environments producing independently verifiable safety certificates. Not self-reported safety claims. SyntheticWatch monitors. Seridor proves.
